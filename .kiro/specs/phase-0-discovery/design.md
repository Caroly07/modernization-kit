# Phase 0: Discovery Interview — Design

## Approach

Conduct the discovery as a structured conversation, not a checklist. Ask questions in groups, follow up based on answers, and let the conversation flow naturally. Some answers trigger domain-specific follow-up questions (e.g., "healthcare" triggers HIPAA questions, "defense" triggers ITAR questions).

## Question Flow

1. Start with Organization & Industry (broad context)
2. Move to Regulatory & Compliance (constraints that affect everything)
3. Application Landscape (what are we working with)
4. Current Pain Points (what hurts)
5. People & Knowledge (who knows what)
6. Existing Infrastructure (what hardware/software already exists)
7. Goals & Constraints (where are we going)

## Key Behaviors

- Accept "I don't know" as a valid and valuable answer — flag it as an open question
- When regulatory frameworks are identified, note them prominently — they carry forward into every subsequent phase
- When infrastructure is inventoried, note anything underutilized — it may change the architecture
- When pain points are described, ask for specifics: frequency, business impact, current workaround
- When technology mandates are mentioned, ask WHY — sometimes mandates are outdated policies that can be revisited

## Output Format

The Discovery Summary should be organized by question group with clear sections. Each section should note:
- What was answered
- What remains unknown (with a plan to find out)
- Implications for the modernization (how this answer affects subsequent phases)
